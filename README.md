## 🤔 What is a Skill?

Think of a skill as a set of instructions you give Claude once, so it knows how to do a specific task really well — every time.

Without a skill, Claude is a great generalist. With a skill, it becomes a specialist.

For example, the **LinkedIn Post Crafter** skill teaches Claude:
- Your personal writing style and tone
- How to anchor posts to recent news
- What phrases you'd never use
- How long your posts should be

Once installed, you just say _"write a LinkedIn post about my promotion"_ and Claude handles the rest — sounding like you, not like a robot.

## 🚀 How to Run a Skill — 2 Ways

There are two ways to use a skill in Claude. Pick whichever suits you.

---

### Method 1 — Via a Project ⭐ Recommended

This is the best option. Once set up, Claude remembers the skill permanently across all future conversations — you never have to paste anything again.

**Step 1 — Copy the skill**
1. Click on the skill folder above (e.g. `linkedin-post-crafter`)
2. Click on `SKILL.md`
3. Click the **Raw** button (top right)
4. Select all (`Ctrl+A` / `Cmd+A`) and copy (`Ctrl+C` / `Cmd+C`)

**Step 2 — Create a Project in Claude**
1. Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) and sign in
2. On the left sidebar, click **"Projects"**
3. Click **"New Project"** and give it a name (e.g. _"LinkedIn Writing"_)
4. Inside the project, click **"Edit project instructions"**
5. Paste (`Ctrl+V` / `Cmd+V`) the skill content
6. Click **Save**

**Step 3 — Start chatting**
Open any conversation inside that project and just talk naturally:
- _"Write a LinkedIn post about completing my first online course."_
- _"Help me post about a team win at work."_

Claude will remember the skill every time you use that project. ✅

---

### Method 2 — Via Customize > Add Skills > Chat

This is the quickest way to try a skill without setting up a project. The skill will be active for as long as it's enabled.

**Step 1 — Copy the skill**
Same as Method 1, Steps 1–4 above.

**Step 2 — Add the skill in Claude settings**
1. Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) and sign in
2. Click your **profile icon** (bottom left)
3. Click **"Settings"**
4. Go to **"Customize Claude"** → look for **"Add Skills"** or **"Custom Instructions"**
5. Paste the skill content in
6. Save

**Step 3 — Start a new chat**
Open a new conversation and start talking — the skill is now active:
- _"Write a LinkedIn post about completing my first online course."_
- _"Help me post about a team win at work."_

> 💡 The difference: **Projects** keep your skill in one dedicated space. **Customize** applies it across all your chats globally.

## 💬 First-Time Experience: What to Expect

When you use the **LinkedIn Post Crafter** for the first time, Claude will ask you a few quick questions:

1. **Your role and audience** — What do you do? Who reads your LinkedIn?
2. **Your tone** — How do you like to come across? (casual, data-driven, storytelling, etc.)
3. **3 sample posts** — This is the most important part:
   - One **long post** (3+ sentences) — a story or experience
   - One **short post** (max 2 sentences) — a quick observation or announcement
   - One **tone reference** — a post (yours or someone else's) that represents how you *want* to sound

After that, Claude builds your personal voice profile and starts writing posts tailored to you.

---

## 🛠️ Tips for Best Results

- **Give real samples** — The messier and more authentic, the better. Claude learns more from how you actually write than from how you think you write.
- **Correct the drafts** — If something sounds off, say so. Even rewriting one sentence yourself teaches Claude your rhythm.
- **Use it regularly** — The more you use it, the more naturally it captures your voice.

## 🙋 FAQ

**Do I need to be a developer to use this?**
Not at all. If you can copy and paste, you're good.

**Does this work on the free version of Claude?**
Yes! Though Projects (for saving skills across chats) may require Claude Pro. If you're on the free plan, just paste the skill at the start of each new conversation.

**Can I customise the skill further?**
Absolutely. Open the `SKILL.md` file, edit any part of it, and re-paste it into Claude. It's just text.

**Can I share this with a friend?**
Yes — just send them this GitHub link. They can follow the same steps above.
